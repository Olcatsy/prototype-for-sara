Land Acknowledgement

I acknowledge the land on which this work was created. It has been a site of human activity for 15,000 years. This land is the traditional territory of the Huron-Wendat and Petun First Nations, the Seneca, and most recently, the Mississaugas of the Credit River. The territory is the subject of the Dish With One Spoon Wampum Belt Covenant, an agreement between the Iroquois Confederacy and Confederacy of the Ojibwe and allied nations to peaceably share and care for the resources around the Great Lakes. The Dish with One Spoon Covenant characterizes our collective responsibility to each other and Mother Earth - to take only what we need, leave enough for others, and keep the dish clean.

Today, the meeting place of Toronto is still the home to many Indigenous people from across Turtle Island. I am grateful to have the opportunity to work in the community, on this territory.

Worm's Eye View

Introduction

[player prompt]

This is a speculation on a worm’s perspective: It’s the future—let’s say the earth has passed around the sun 88 888 888 times since the year 2020.

Imagine yourself a researcher—a Terrestrial Isopod (Pillbug), safe underground, exploring the many systems buried just a few feet under your computer. Instead of favouring familiar anthropocentric classification systems, the information you uncover is processed through scent, vibrations, oscillations, colour patterns. Sometimes, objects are identified through echolocation.

You can switch cursors to navigate other subterranean realms. Much can be learned from these alternative perspectives. Perhaps the information of the objects you unearth can be processed and relayed back to other arthropod cousins through dance or multivariate data visualizations.

Your cursor becomes a bird’s clawed foot, a hoofed hind leg, the delicate tarsus of a cricket’s arm, an antique gardening glove—an old relic from the early Holocene… use it to dig into the virtual subterrestrial world of fungi, slime mould, insects, gas lines, plumbing, cables, discarded objects.

User Guide

  1. Scratch the surface of the dig site with your cursor to begin digging a hole. As you dig, you will encounter natural, immaterial, and human-made phenomena.

  2. *Tip while digging*. In some instances, you may find it helpful to move the cursor more, clockwise and counterclockwise over an entity to remove their surface layer.

  3. A yellow halo will appear to indicate that an entity is fully uncovered. Click on the entity to unearth it. It will be transported to the edge of the dig site.

  4. Excavate all embedded entities from the current layer to move on to the new one underneath.

  5. All entities within the hole contain additional embedded text. Click on them to access this information once they are unearthed.

  6. Switch between the three alternative realms by clicking on elements from the “choose your realm” section at the bottom left corner. Your progress on the current realm will be saved.

  7. Proceed to the next realm once you unearthed all entities on the current one.Continue the process as long as you like (you eventually win worm).

  8. Refreshing the browser will reset the process.
